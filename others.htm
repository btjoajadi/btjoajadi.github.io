Other Things to do in Bali

Ku De Ta
Ku De Ta is a highly acclaimed and immensely popular beachfront lounge in Bali. Located in southern Bali along the Seminyak Beach, Ku De Ta is known for its picturesque views. Visitors of Bali would often conclude their day in Ku De Ta, watching the sunset and enjoying the variety of snacks and drinks available at the bar while listening to Ku De Ta. Ku De Ta is also home to Mejekawi, a restaurant that serves gourmet local Balinese and Indonesian food.
Kuta Beachwalk
Kuta Beachwalk is a mere minute away from one of the most, if not the most, well known beach in Bali: Kuta Beach. The mall itself has an array of shops, both local and multinational, that is difficult to find in other parts of Bali. The building itself is designed to be sustainable, and includes an open atrium at the center of the mall that provides natural air flow and a garden. Visitors come to enjoy a brief return to reality while still being in touch with the special escape Bali provides.
Bounty Discotheque
 Bounty Discotheque is a popular destination for tourists in search of a wild and rachet clubbing scene. Famous for its wet 'n' wild parties, Bounty Discotheque is most popular with local Indonesian and Australian tourists. It is a mix of the traditional discotheque and the modern club,providing the layout of a discotheque, along with the expansive dance floor and a disco ball, and the music, playlist, and DJ often present in modern clubs.
Rock Bar Bali
 Rated one of the best Beach bars in the world by CNN Travel, Rock Bar is undoubtedly one of the most popular destinations in Bali. Visitors are able to enjoy the view of the beach underneath them while sitting and sipping their drinks atop a cliffside. Although a dresscode is required to enter the bar, it gives a classy, exclusive feeling upon entering the bar itself. Famous for its sunset views, Rock Bar also features great ambience for romantic rendez-vous.
Ubud Monkey Forest
 Ubud Monkey Forest is known as one of Ubud's most popular attraction for those looking to experience something other than the beaches of Bali. Surrounded by a forest filled with monkeys, tourists can visit multiple temples all throughout the forest. Although it is not a must, the monkey forest urges its visitors to avoid wearing loose jewelry as the mischievous monkeys will snatch them away from its owners. Guides are also available at the site should visitors want to learn more about the sacred forest.
